Compact 5-Week Female Packing List

Packing for Thailand, Taiwan, Japan, and Korea in a duffel and a day backpack.

Posted on June 24, 2019

Based on my prior experiences lugging a giant suitcase up and down subways in Asia, I wanted to make my life easier to take with me as I traversed 8 cities.

My Situation: 5-Week Trip to Eastern Asia between late September to early November. A quick Google search told me the weather in Thailand was still hot and humid, but the temperatures would get progressively colder as the trip went on. My partner and I did a lot of sightseeing around the city, so plenty of walking, but no intense nature adventures. We didn’t have fancy events to go to either (maybe when we have more money). I did purchase an extra jacket and more fall clothing in Japan and Korea because the fall weather was colder than my southern California self expected. I also have a mild shopping problem, but what I packed got me through over half of the trip.

Here's my list!

Packing Vessels:

Skyway Whidby Duffel

  • This duffel carried 90% of what I initially packed, and held all the souvenirs I brought back, along with some of my partner’s items. It’s got plenty of pockets, a nice thick fabric, and it comes in a cute color. I didn’t want a black duffel because it would’ve been difficult to spot in a sea of black luggage in the event I ever needed to check the bag in (which I only did on my return trip). The shoulder strap is also detachable!

Kipling City Pack

  • I carried my daily essentials and important items (passport, wallet, etc) around with me everywhere I went in this backpack. It has a lot of room and pockets and it’s made well!

Packing Cubes

  • Amazon has tons of brands. I just used what my mom had at home.


Clothing

6 Tops

  • 2 t-shirts made of athletic material (Athleta and Old Navy)
  • 1 basic cotton tee (Everlane)
  • 1 mid sleeve blouse (Uniqlo)
  • 1 long sleeve shirt (Costco)
  • 1 tank top for sleeping

3 Pants - I like skinny, tight-fitting bottoms so change accordingly for you comfort!

  • 1 pair of light-wash jeggings (Old Navy-similar)
  • 2 leggings - I wear leggings all the time and they are compact. (Girlfriend Collective and Old Navy)

2 Shorts

  • 1 pair of denim shorts
  • 1 pair of running shorts - doubles as PJ pants (Old Navy)

2 Outerwear - this is where I underestimated “fall weather” outside of SoCal

  • 1 pullover sweater (Gymshark)
  • 1 light sports jacket (Target)

2 Pairs of Shoes

  • 1 pair of sneakers (Nike
  • 1 pair of comfy sandals (Clarks)

4 Bras - this totally depends on you preference

  • 2 sports bras (Gymshark and Puma)
  • 2 t-shirt bras (Maidenform)

7 undies (mix of bikini cut and thongs)

5 socks (mix of low rise and crew socks)



Toiletries

  • Jojoba Oil (face moisturizer and makeup remover)
  • Toner spray
  • Bar soap (body wash and mentrual blood remover)
  • Small amount of mulitpurpose soap
  • Menstrual cup
  • Contacts case and contact solution (travel size)
  • Foldable toothbrush
  • Lip MedEx (for nighttime use)
  • Nail clippers + filer
  • Tweezers
  • Mini hair brush
  • Plastic hairties (pack of 100 from Daiso)
  • Floss picks

Makeup


  • Lip Balm
  • Eyebrow pencil
  • Liquid eyeliner
  • Single eyeshadow
  • Lip crayon

Medicine/First Aid

  • Allergy medicine (Allegra+Benadryl)
  • Motrin
  • Birth control pills
  • Sunblock Lotion (I like this because it's not greasy)
  • Lip crayon
  • Pepto Bismol (chewables)
  • Neosporin
  • Band-aids
  • VICK's minty inhaling thing
  • Tea tree oil (for mosquito repellent)
  • Eyedrops (contacts+medicinal)

Electronics


  • Portable charger
  • Earphones
  • Phone charger
  • Outlet converter (cheap one from Amazon)
  • Fitbit + charger

Misc.

  • Pocket mirror
  • Mosquito repellent wipes, bracelets, and patches
  • Quick dry mini towel
  • Sketchbook + pens
  • Neck pillow
  • Passport + scanned copies
  • Coin purse (essential in Taiwan and Japan)
  • Pack of tissues
  • Foldable tote bag (similar)
  • Wallet
  • Glasses

And there you go! It’s not a perfect packing list but it worked really well for me. A lot of the items are things I already owned, so I didn’t go out to buy too many things specifically for this trip (i’m poor y‘know). I did have to control myself in terms of shopping in 8 cities, but I had plenty of space to buy smaller souvenirs! I also booked Airbnb places with washing machines (dryer’s don’t really exist in Asia LOL)

I learned a lot from my experience and I definitely think I can do better with a few tweaks next time, like being more prepared for colder weather. Although I did buy clothes along the way, the items I packed were still the core of my wardrobe since the colors were basic and the fabris were high quality and made for comfort+adventure.
